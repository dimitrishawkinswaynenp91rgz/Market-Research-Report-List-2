<p><p>Portable ophthalmic ultrasound systems refer to medical devices that use sound waves to create images of the eye's internal structures. There are several types of portable ophthalmic ultrasound systems, including A-Scan, which measures the length of the eye and is used to calculate intraocular lens power, B-Scan, which produces 2D images of the eye's anatomy, Combined Scan, which combines A-Scan and B-Scan capabilities, Pachymeter, which measures corneal thickness, and Ultrasound Bio-microscopy (UBM), which provides detailed images of the anterior segment of the eye.</p></p>
