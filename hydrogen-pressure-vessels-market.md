<p><p>Hydrogen pressure vessels are categorized into four types based on the materials used and design specifications. Type I vessels are made of steel and have the highest weight and thickness. Type II vessels are constructed using a combination of steel and fiber-reinforced polymers. Type III vessels feature a metal liner wrapped with carbon or glass fibers. Type IV vessels are made entirely of composite materials, making them the lightest and most expensive option. These different types cater to various applications and requirements in the hydrogen industry.</p></p>
