<p><ul><li>Through-hole</li><li>Chassis Mount</li><li>Surface Mount</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Wire wound power resistors are divided into three main types based on their mounting method - through-hole, chassis mount, and surface mount. Through-hole resistors are mounted by inserting their leads through holes in a circuit board and soldering them in place. Chassis mount resistors are designed to be mounted on the chassis or enclosure of an electronic device. Surface mount resistors are mounted directly onto the surface of a circuit board using solder paste. Each type has its own advantages and is used in different applications based on requirements.</p></p>
