<p><p>Large Aperture Scintillometer market types include Plano-convex Lens and Fresnel Lens. The Plano-convex Lens scintillometer utilizes a lens with one flat surface and one convex surface to focus the light onto a detector, allowing for accurate measurements of atmospheric turbulence. On the other hand, the Fresnel Lens scintillometer uses a thin lens with concentric grooves to focus light, providing a compact design and efficient light collection. Both types offer different advantages based on their design and application requirements in the market.</p></p>
