<p><p>Piezoelectric disk market types include unimorph and bimorph configurations. Unimorph piezoelectric disks consist of a single layer of piezoelectric material bonded to a metal backing, allowing for one-dimensional movement when activated. In contrast, bimorph piezoelectric disks consist of two layers of piezoelectric material with opposite polarity, allowing for two-dimensional movement when activated. Both unimorph and bimorph configurations are used in a variety of applications such as sensors, actuators, and energy harvesting devices due to their ability to convert mechanical energy into electrical energy.</p></p>
