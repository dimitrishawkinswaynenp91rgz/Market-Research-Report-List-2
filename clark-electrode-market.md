<p><p>A Clark Electrode is a type of electrochemical sensor primarily used in the measurement of dissolved oxygen in liquid samples. It consists of a platinum cathode and a silver anode, separated by an electrolyte solution. When oxygen molecules diffuse through the membrane of the electrode, it reacts with the cathode to produce an electric current, which is proportional to the amount of oxygen present in the sample.</p><p>The Clark Electrode Market is experiencing significant growth due to the increasing demand for accurate and reliable oxygen measurement in various industries such as healthcare, environmental monitoring, and biotechnology.
