<p><p>Raising agents play a crucial role in baking to help dough or batter rise and become light and airy. The three main types of raising agents are yeast, baking powder, and baking soda. Yeast works by fermenting sugars in the dough to produce carbon dioxide gas. Baking powder contains both an acid and a base that react when mixed with liquid, producing carbon dioxide. Baking soda, or sodium bicarbonate, also produces carbon dioxide when combined with an acidic ingredient. Other types of raising agents include cream of tartar and self-rising flour.</p></p>
