<p><p>A bunionectomy is a surgical procedure used to correct a bunion, a bony bump that forms on the joint at the base of the big toe. There are three main types of bunionectomy procedures: osteotomy, exostectomy, and arthrodesis. Osteotomy involves cutting and realigning the bone, exostectomy involves removing the bony bump, and arthrodesis involves fusing the joint to improve its alignment. Each type of bunionectomy targets different aspects of the condition and is selected based on individual patient needs.</p></p>
